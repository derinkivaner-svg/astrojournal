# Astrojournal

Overview
AstroJournal is a personal astrology journal that connects to your natal chart and shows you the sky as it relates to you specifically. Paste your chart data from Astro-Seek and the app calculates real transits, ingresses, and moon phases, and uses them to generate daily journaling prompts, weekly overviews, and seasonal readings personalized to your planetary placements.
The core idea is simple: astrology is most useful when it meets you where you actually are. AstroJournal is not a generic horoscope generator. It reads your chart and the current sky together, every day, and gives you something worth writing about.

Getting Started
Step 1: Get your chart from Astro-Seek
AstroJournal uses Astro-Seek to get your natal chart data. Here is how to export it:
Go to astro-seek.com and click Birth Chart in the top navigation.
Enter your birth date, exact birth time, and birth city.
Once your chart loads, scroll down to the planet and house tables below the wheel.
Select and copy all the text in the tables. This should include all planetary positions with signs, degrees, and house placements, all twelve house cusps, and the list of aspects.
You do not need to copy the chart image itself, only the text data.
Note: Birth time accuracy matters. An exact birth time gives you the correct Ascendant, Midheaven, and house placements. If you only have an approximate time, your house cusps may be slightly off, but planetary positions will still be correct.
Step 2: Paste into AstroJournal
When you open AstroJournal for the first time, you will see the onboarding screen. Paste the text you copied from Astro-Seek into the input field and click Parse Chart.
The app will read your chart and show you a confirmation screen listing everything it found: each planet with its sign, degree, and house, all twelve house cusps, and your major aspects. Review this to make sure everything looks correct before proceeding.
Once confirmed, your chart is saved locally on your device. You will not need to re-enter it unless you reset the app.

Features
Calendar View
The Calendar is the home screen of AstroJournal. It shows the full current month with real astrological events displayed directly on each day cell, similar to how Google Calendar or iCal show events.
Each day cell shows:
The moon phase emoji for that day
Colored event pills for any astrological events on that date
A gold dot if you have a saved journal entry for that day
Event pill colors indicate event type:
Silver and white for New Moons and Full Moons
Purple for planetary ingresses (a planet entering a new sign)
Amber for retrograde stations, both Rx begin and direct
Gold for Sun sign changes
Click any day to open that date in the Day view and see your full reading for it. Navigate between months using the arrow buttons at the top of the calendar.
Day View
The Day view is the heart of AstroJournal. Select any date and the app generates a full personalized reading for that day in four sections:
Astrological Climate
A concise description of what is happening in the sky today and how the active transits aspect your natal chart. This is not a generic daily horoscope. It references your specific planets and houses by name.
Journaling Prompt
One focused, personal question to write about that day. The prompt is rooted in your natal chart themes and the current sky, so it shifts with each day as transits change. It appears in a styled callout box with a gold left border. This is the feature that makes the journal feel like yours specifically.
Soul Guidance
Two to three sentences of practical guidance for the day. This section connects your North Node direction with what the current transits are asking of you. It is written to be actionable and grounded, not abstract.
Affirmation
One short statement to carry through the day, drawn from your Sun sign and nodal axis.
Below the reading is your journal entry space. Write as much or as little as you want. Use the Save button to keep your entry, and the Copy for Apple Journal button to format everything, the reading plus your entry, cleanly for pasting into Apple Journal or Notes.
Week View
The Week view gives you a bird's-eye view of the current seven-day period. It shows:
A weekly overview paragraph describing the overall energy and how it touches your chart
A Focus card highlighting the single most important theme or opportunity this week
A Navigate Carefully card noting what to be mindful of, specific to your placements
A day-by-day breakdown with the planetary day ruler and a one-sentence energy note for each day
A weekly journaling prompt to sit with across the whole week
Use the arrows at the top to move between weeks.
Season View
The Season view gives you the big picture for the current astronomical season, whether that is Spring, Summer, Autumn, or Winter. The reading is divided into labeled sections:
Season Overview: Season Overview
North Node Path: How the season activates or challenges your North Node path
Career and Studio: Career and creative work themes
Relationships and Family: Relationship and family dynamics
Body and Energy: Body and energy guidance
Key Dates and Windows: Notable astrological events and timing windows to watch
Seasonal Intention: One clear intention for the season rooted in your evolutionary path
You can regenerate the seasonal reading at any time using the Regenerate button at the bottom of the view.
Interactive Chart
The interactive chart displays your full natal wheel with hover functionality. When you move your cursor over any element in the chart, a tooltip appears with a brief description of what that placement or aspect means.
Hover interactions are available for:
Each planet in its sign and house, with a short interpretation of what that combination means in your chart
Each aspect line between planets, with a description of the nature of that aspect and how the two planets interact
Each house cusp, with a quick summary of what that house governs in astrology
This is designed as a quick-reference tool, not a full reading. It is useful when you are learning to read your own chart and want to understand what you are looking at without having to leave the app.

Journaling and Export
Every day view includes a freeform text area for your journal entry. Entries are saved locally on your device under the date they were written. Saved dates appear with a gold dot on the calendar so you can see at a glance which days you have written.
Copy for Apple Journal
The Copy for Apple Journal button formats your full entry for clean pasting into Apple Journal, Notes, or any other writing app. The exported text includes:
The date and day of the week
The moon phase and planetary day ruler
The astrological climate reading
The journaling prompt
The soul guidance
The affirmation if one was generated
Your written journal entry
The formatting uses plain text so it pastes cleanly without any app-specific styling.

Settings
Click the gear icon in the top right corner to open the settings panel. Options include:
Reset Chart: clears your stored chart data and returns you to the onboarding screen. Use this if you want to update your chart or correct a parsing error.
Clear All Journal Entries: permanently removes all saved journal entries from local storage. This cannot be undone.
Time Display: toggle between 12-hour and 24-hour time format.

Data and Privacy
AstroJournal stores everything locally on your device using your browser's localStorage. Your chart data and journal entries are never sent to any external server. The app does not require an account and does not collect any personal information.
Because data is stored in localStorage, it is tied to the browser and device you are using. Clearing your browser data or switching browsers will remove your stored chart and entries. If you want to keep your entries, use the Copy for Apple Journal function regularly to back them up to a writing app.

Tips for Getting the Most Out of AstroJournal
Write even on low-energy days. The prompt is there to make it easier to start. Two sentences is enough.
Check the Calendar at the start of each month to see what astrological events are coming and mentally prepare for the bigger ones like full moons, eclipses, and retrograde stations.
Use the Week view on Sunday evenings to orient yourself to the energy of the week ahead.
The Season view is best read at the start of each new season and then returned to occasionally as a reminder of the bigger picture.
Hover over your chart when something in a reading references a placement you are not familiar with. The tooltips are there as a reference, not a replacement for study.
The journaling prompt is the most valuable part of the daily view. Even if you skip writing the full entry, sit with the prompt for a moment before your day begins.
